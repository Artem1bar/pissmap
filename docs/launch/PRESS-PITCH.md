# PissMap NOLA — Press Pitch Kit

Every number below is computed live from the dataset (`lib/stats.ts`), not
guessed. As of this writing: **412 spots · 37 neighborhoods · 43 open 24/7 ·
95 no-purchase · 100 wheelchair-accessible** (67 free & public, 317 buy-something,
28 hotel-lobby). Re-run `npm run preflight` and check `/press` before quoting —
if the dataset grew, so did the numbers.

## The 150-word core pitch

New Orleans hands you a drink for the street and then offers you nowhere to put
it. **PissMap NOLA** is the fix: an interactive dark map of **412 field-vetted
places to pee** across the city — free public restrooms, cheap-coffee spots, and
hotel lobbies for the bold — spanning **37 neighborhoods**, with **43 open
around the clock**.

Every pin was adversarially fact-checked before it earned a place. A live
"open-now" engine tells you what's actually unlocked at 2 a.m.; one **GOTTA
GEAUX** button ranks the nearest open restroom by walking time. Reviews are
anonymous and human-moderated; when a spot goes stale, tourists flag it and it's
patched the same day.

It's free, has no ads, needs no account, and exists so you never have to test how
citable public urination really is in this town. Laissez les bons temps rouler —
responsibly.

## Tailored angles

**NOLA.com / The Times-Picayune** — the civic-service angle. "A local built the
public-restroom map the city never did." Lead with the free-and-public count
(67 spaces you can walk into with no purchase), library and market restrooms, and
the accessibility data (100 wheelchair-accessible spots). Human-interest, useful,
screenshot-friendly.

**Gambit** — the irreverent-but-real angle. Gambit readers get the joke *and*
want the utility. Lean into the voice ("when you gotta geaux"), the go-cup
culture tie-in, and the Carnival survival use-case. This is the outlet most
likely to run the honest, funny version.

**Eater New Orleans** — the food angle. 317 of the spots are buy-something
venues: cafés, diners, food halls. Frame it as "the cheapest coffee is the price
of admission" — a guide to which counter-service spots welcome a $3 order and a
bathroom run. Pair with the frozen-Irish-coffee-toll gag.

**WWL / Fox 8 (broadcast)** — the practical-TV-segment angle. Visual, phone-first,
demo-able live: pull it up, hit GOTTA GEAUX, watch it route you. Emphasize
Mardi Gras / festival season prep and the 43 always-open options. Great for a
"news you can use" evening hit.

**Very Local** — the neighborhood-guide angle. 37 neighborhoods, each with at
least one vetted spot. Pitch a "where to go in [neighborhood]" explainer series
built from the map. Evergreen, shareable, hyper-local.
